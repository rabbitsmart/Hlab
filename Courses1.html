Introduction to Psychology

Course Objective

Psychology is a laboratory-based science investigating the human mind and behavior. This course is a broad introduction to the field of psychology. We will review several subfields of psychology such as sensation and perception, cognition, neuroscience, social psychology, developmetal psychology, biological psychology, and clinical psychology. Studnets in this course will learn the key concepts, perspectives and research findings that shaped contemporary psychology



Materials and Reference

Psycholgoical Science, 5/e (IE) Michael Gazzaniga, Todd Teatherton, Diane Halpern



Evaluation Method

-Attendance/Quizzes: 10%
-Midterm Exam: 30%
-Final Exam: 40%
-Assignment1: 10%
-Assignment2: 10%

Attendance Policy
Studnets who are absent for over 1/3 of the course will receive a grade of 'F' or 'U' for the course
(Exceptions can be made when the cause of absence is deemed unavoidable by the course instructor).

Exam Policy
Exams will consist of multiple choice questions. Midterm (60 questions) & Final (80 questions)
